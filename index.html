Newton's Second Law
A net force F acting on a mass m produces a constant acceleration for that step:
a = F / m
Because a is constant within each step, the SUVAT displacement equation applies directly:
x = x₀ + u·t + ½·a·t²
Here u is the velocity at the start of the step and t is the time elapsed within it. Each step passes its final velocity to the next as the new u.
Now pick a landmark (or multiple) to visit. Determine the distance you want to move and substitute acceleration using the mass and force expression above. Decide how fast you want to get there and calculate the force required. Note that you will probably want to move half the distance under one force, then apply an equal and opposite force to decelerate for the second half.
